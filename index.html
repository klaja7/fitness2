Injury Prevention

Stretching, mobility, speed preservation & tips for a soccer player with cervical hernias

⚡ Protecting & Building Your Speed
Don't build bulky legs
Heavy high-volume quad work adds mass that slows acceleration. The lower body program keeps volume intentionally moderate — enough to strengthen, not enough to bulk.
Train explosiveness, not just strength
From Phase 2 onward, add 2–3 sets of box jumps or broad jumps before your lower body session (when fresh). 3×5 is enough — don't do these on tired legs.
Hip flexor strength = sprint speed
Weak hip flexors are the most underrated limiter of top speed. The hanging knee raises and dead bugs in the program address this directly.
Nordic Curls protect your speed
Nordic Curls build eccentric hamstring strength — the exact quality that prevents hamstring tears at full sprint. In the program from Phase 1 and never optional.
Don't run yourself flat in Phase 3
Keep at least one run as short sprints or strides (6–8×80m at 85–90% effort with full recovery). This maintains fast-twitch fiber recruitment that long slow runs erode.
🌅 Daily Mobility — every morning (10–15 min)
Hip Flexor Stretch (kneeling)
Daily
2×45s per side
Rear knee on ground, drive hips forward, squeeze rear glute. Critical for soccer players — tight hip flexors reduce stride length and strain the lower back.
90/90 Hip Stretch
Daily
2×45s per side
Sit on floor with both legs at 90° angles, lean over front shin. Opens hip rotation range — the movement used most in cutting and turning at speed.
Thoracic Rotation (seated)
Daily
2×10/side
Sit cross-legged, hands behind head, rotate torso. When thoracic spine stiffens, the cervical spine compensates — this prevents that transfer.
World's Greatest Stretch
Pre-workout
5 reps/side
Lunge forward, same-side hand on ground, opposite arm rotates to ceiling. Best single pre-workout primer — hits hip flexor, thoracic, and hamstring together.
Standing Hamstring Stretch
Daily
2×40s per side
Foot on low surface, slight forward hinge at hip. Hamstring flexibility directly influences sprint mechanics and reduces injury risk at top speed.
Calf & Achilles Stretch
Daily
2×40s per side
Against wall: straight leg for gastroc, bent knee for Achilles. Soccer players neglect this constantly — 90 seconds daily prevents a lot of pain.
🔥 Pre-Workout Activation — every gym session (5 min)
Glute Bridge
2×15 reps
Activates glutes before lower body work. Dormant glutes shift load to hamstrings and lower back.
Scapular Push-ups
2×12 reps
Protract/retract scapula without bending elbows. Primes the shoulder before pressing and protects the cervical chain.
Chin Tucks
2×15 reps
Always before upper body. Resets cervical posture and activates deep neck flexors before any shoulder loading.
Arm Circles + Shoulder CARs
10 reps each direction
Slow controlled articular rotations through full range. Reduces shoulder impingement risk during pressing.
🧊 Recovery Tips
🛁
Cold shower after intense sessions
2–3 minutes cold water on worked muscles after Phase 2 and 3 sessions. Reduces inflammation and speeds recovery.
😴
Sleep is your best recovery tool
7.5–9 hours. Growth hormone peaks in the first 90 minutes of deep sleep. Even one hour less measurably reduces next-day strength output.
🦵
Foam roll calves, quads & IT band 2–3x/week
Not daily. Focus on calves and lateral quad. Reduces stiffness and maintains tissue quality across the 10 weeks.
🚶
Walk on rest days
20–30 minutes easy walking on off-days keeps blood flowing, reduces soreness, adds no meaningful fatigue.
📅
Deload week — Week 5
Drop all sets by 40%, remove heaviest compound sets, keep every exercise and frequency the same. You will come out of it stronger.
🔴 Bad Neck Day — Exercise Substitutions

Increased stiffness, arm weakness, or tingling? Don't skip the gym — just swap these:

Cable Lateral Raise only — no overhead pressing
Barbell Row
Chest-supported DB Row on incline bench
Weighted Pull-up
Bodyweight pull-up or assisted pull-up only
Skull Crusher
Tricep pushdown (cable) — no neck extension under load
Any pressing feeling wrong
Replace entire push session with face pulls, lateral raises, and core only
Lat Pulldown feeling off
Seated cable row with neutral grip — less cervical involvement

Neck Safety Protocol

Non-negotiable throughout all 10 weeks.

01No overhead pressing behind the neck — ever
02Avoid heavy axial barbell loading — use goblet squat, safety bar, or leg press instead if any symptoms arise
03No extreme neck flexion or extension under load
04Cable Face Pulls every single session — most protective exercise for cervical discs
05If any tingling or shooting pain occurs in arm/shoulder, stop immediately — not after the set
06Supported rows (chest on bench) preferred over free-standing bent-over rows
07Lat Pulldown: front only — never behind the neck
08Sleep with a cervical support pillow
